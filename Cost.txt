100. University of Kentucky
The University of Kentucky (UK) in Lexington, founded in 1865, is a public land-grant research university. With 32,710 students (fall 2022), it's the largest enrollment institution in Kentucky. Offering diverse programs across 16 colleges and a graduate school, it's classified as "R1: Doctoral Universities – Very high research activity." In 2018, Kentucky spent $393 million on research and development, ranking 63rd nationally (National Science Foundation). The William T. Young Library is the largest of seven libraries on campus. Emphasizing research, the university aims to be a Top 20 public research institution by 2020. Notably, two alumni have received Nobel Prizes.
